Research

My research focuses on sustainable space systems — from microwave-assisted lunar recycling and high power microwave propulsion environments to digital twin modeling and mission concept development.

High Power Microwave Systems for Lunar Missions

At the Space Propulsion and Environments Laboratory, I researched high power microwave (HPM) systems for future long-term lunar missions. I developed a technical proposal for a lunar recycling system integrated within a centralized microwave network.

  • Subsystem-level systems design for lunar recycling architecture
  • Novel catalyst selections to increase methane generation from plastic waste
  • Feasibility analysis for in-situ rocket propellant production on the Moon

NASA LunaRecycle Challenge

As Research Lead (Phase 1) and Chief Engineer (Phase 2), I directed research and engineering efforts for Penn State's LunaRecycle team competing in NASA's challenge to recycle waste on the lunar surface.

  • MATLAB heat transfer model improving testing efficiency by ~20%
  • Microwave-assisted plastic pyrolysis proof-of-concept system
  • $50,000 Milestone Round award and finalist round qualification
  • MBSE-driven reviews from SCR through CDR

Digital Twin for RECLAIM

I developed a Digital Twin model for the RECLAIM system that links COMSOL thermal simulations, real-time LabVIEW sensor data, and CAD visualizations directly to MBSE requirements — enabling traceable verification throughout the design lifecycle.
